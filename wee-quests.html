Wee Quests: UX and UI Design

Project Overview

The aim of this project was to implement user-centred design practices to build an interactive prototype for a concept web app.

I completed the project for my UX Certification with CareerFoundry and created Wee Quests, a scavenger hunt app. Exploratory research showed people needed motivation to go out and take a scavenger hunt and indicated that my target audience would respond well to an app with a friendly, helpful tone of voice.

Based on this hypothesis, I created a mascot to guide people through tasks on the app. Usability testing confirmed that the app’s personality was good for motivation but also revealed that at times people found the app lacking in freedom.

The final design aims to create a sense of fun and balance my audience’s need for readily available guidance with their need for freedom to explore the app.

A Snapshot of my Design Process:

Prototyping and Testing: Developing Wee Quests' Personality and Resolving Issues

1. User research showed that my target audience would respond well to an app with a friendly, helpful tone of voice

2. I began to develop a personality for the app that would assist users with onboarding tips as they created their first scavenger hunt

3. I brainstormed ideas for a mascot that would embody the cheerful personality I wanted Wee Quests to have as well as the app’s themes of Scotland and adventure

4. With my mascot design ready, I created screen mock-ups and put them together into a clickable prototype

5. Usability testing revealed that users felt lost on the first Create a Hunt screen without a prompt. They didn’t know what to do at first. One participant remarked ‘Sorry, I’ve broken your app!’

6. In response to the issue of users feeling lost, I introduced a tips icon as well as a banner to show the current task and in-app location

7. The tips icon also aims to tackle another issue: although test participants really liked the mascot, they felt the Create a Hunt process was sometimes lacking in freedom: the tips icon means that guidance is now optional

Refining the Design: Updates to Wee Quests' Personality and Design Hierarchy

1. Building on positive feedback in testing, I further developed the app’s personality. The way the mascot’s poses interact with UI elements is designed to add engagement and a sense of fun

2. I updated the language on call outs to be more conversational and to allow users to make proactive choices

3. Meanwhile, I updated UI elements where necessary to ensure a strong and consistent hierarchy that would help guide users through tasks. Here, I want the user’s first instinct on the second screen to be to add another stop and not to preview the hunt

4. Following peer review, I moved the introduction of the tips feature to the beginning of the Create a Hunt feature. Therefore users are onboarded early and tips are optional by the time users are further into the process

5. The overall aim is to strike the balance between my audience’s need for tutorials and the need to give users freedom as they explore the app
